What is an Interest-Only Loan?

An interest-only (IO) loan is a type of debt where the borrower is required to pay only the interest on the principal balance for a set term. During this phase, the principal balance does not decrease unless voluntary extra payments are made.

How It Works

Interest-only loans typically consist of two phases:

  1. Interest-Only Period: You pay only the interest accrued each month. Your monthly obligation is lower, but you build no equity.
  2. Amortization Period: Once the IO term ends, the loan converts. You must now pay both interest and principal over the remaining years of the loan. This often results in a significant "payment shock."

Strategic Use Cases

IO loans are often used by investors who prioritize short-term cash flow or by individuals who expect their income to rise significantly before the amortization period begins. However, they carry higher risks if property values drop or if the borrower is unprepared for the eventual payment increase.

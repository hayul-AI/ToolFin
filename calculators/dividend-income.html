What is Dividend Income?

Dividends are payments made by a corporation to its shareholders, usually as a distribution of profits. When a corporation earns a profit or surplus, it is able to pay a proportion of the profit as a dividend to shareholders.

Dividend income is a popular form of passive income, especially for long-term investors and retirees. The "Dividend Yield" is a key metric that tells you how much a company pays out in dividends each year relative to its stock price.

What Is the Debt Snowball Method?

The Debt Snowball is a debt reduction strategy where you pay off your debts in order from smallest balance to largest balance. Regardless of interest rates, you focus all your extra financial resources on the smallest debt while maintaining minimum payments on everything else.

Once the smallest debt is paid in full, you take the entire amount you were paying toward it (the minimum payment plus the extra) and "roll" it into the payment for the next smallest debt. This process continues, with the payment amount growing larger—like a snowball rolling down a hill—as each debt is eliminated.

Psychological Momentum: Unlike the Debt Avalanche method (which prioritizes highest interest rates), the Snowball focuses on human behavior. By seeing debts disappear quickly at the start, users often feel a sense of accomplishment that helps them stay committed to their long-term plan.

Note: This calculator compares numeric estimates and does not account for specific bank fees, late penalties, or floating interest rates.

FAQ

Is this a guaranteed payoff schedule?

No. This is a mathematical projection based on the numbers you provide. Changes in interest rates, new charges, or missed payments will alter the actual timeline.

Does it include fees or penalties?

Standard calculators focus on principal and interest. It does not account for annual credit card fees, late payment penalties, or balance transfer fees.

What if my APR changes?

Most credit cards have variable APRs. If your rate increases, a larger portion of your payment will go toward interest, potentially extending your payoff date.

What’s the difference between snowball and avalanche?

The Snowball method prioritizes smallest balances first for psychological wins. The Avalanche method prioritizes highest interest rates first to minimize total interest paid.

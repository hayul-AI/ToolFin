How Loan Payments Are Calculated

Fixed-term loans typically use an amortization formula to determine a constant monthly payment. This payment is split into two parts: interest on the remaining balance and principal reduction.

In the early stages of a loan, a larger portion of your payment goes toward interest. As you pay down the principal, the interest charged each month decreases, allowing more of your payment to go toward the balance.

By making extra payments, you directly reduce the principal balance, which can significantly shorten the loan term and reduce the total interest paid over time.

Why calculate your pay raise?

A pay raise is more than just a higher annual number. Breaking it down into monthly or weekly increments helps you understand how it will practically affect your lifestyle and budget. For example, a $5,000 raise might seem large, but knowing it results in roughly $192 extra per bi-weekly paycheck (before taxes) provides better context for planning.

Remember that a higher gross salary might also push you into a higher tax bracket or affect your eligibility for certain tax credits, so the "net" gain may be slightly different than the "gross" gain shown here.
